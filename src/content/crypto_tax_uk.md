# Crypto Tax UK: The Definitive Guide 2026

Welcome to the most comprehensive guide to navigating cryptocurrency taxation in the United Kingdom for the 2026 tax year. As digital assets become increasingly integrated into the financial landscape, HMRC has significantly ramped up its monitoring and enforcement capabilities.

## The Core Principle: Capital Gains vs. Income Tax

In the UK, most crypto transactions fall under **Capital Gains Tax (CGT)**. You pay tax on the "gain"—the difference between what you paid for the asset and what you sold it for.

### When CGT Applies:
* Selling crypto for fiat (GBP, USD, etc.)
* Exchanging one crypto asset for another (e.g., BTC to ETH)
* Using crypto to pay for goods or services
* Gifting crypto to someone other than your spouse or civil partner

### When Income Tax Applies:
* **Staking Rewards:** Often treated as miscellaneous income.
* **Mining:** Treated as trading income if done at scale.
* **Airdrops:** May be taxable depending on the circumstances.
* **Salary paid in Crypto:** Taxed as employment income.

## The 2026 "Annual Exempt Amount"
For the 2025/26 tax year, the CGT allowance remains at **£3,000**. This means the first £3,000 of your total annual capital gains (from all assets, not just crypto) are tax-free.

## Proprietary Strategy: The "Section 104" Pool
HMRC requires you to use "pool" accounting for your assets. This prevents you from "cherry-picking" specific coins to sell for tax advantages. Every time you buy a specific coin, it goes into your Section 104 pool for that asset, and the cost basis is averaged.
